Districts

Wayanad

Wayanad, with its rolling hills, lush tea and spice plantations, and pristine forests, is a mesmerizing natural paradise that captivates visitors with its serene beauty.

Kannur

Kannur, with its pristine beaches and vibrant Theyyam performances, offers a unique blend of natural coastal beauty and rich cultural traditions!



View More

Calicut

Calicut, with its sandy beaches and charming harbor, invites visitors to relax by the Arabian Sea and enjoy stunning sunsets.



View More

Palakkad

Palakkad, known as the "Gateway of Kerala," welcomes you with lush green landscapes and rolling hills.The Western Ghats surrounding Palakkad offer breathtaking vistas and numerous trekking opportunities for nature enthusiasts.



View More

Idukki

Idukki's picturesque hill stations are draped in emerald green tea plantations and provide a cool and refreshing retreat.The district's pristine forests and wildlife sanctuaries make Idukki a paradise for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts.



View More

Ernakulam

Ernakulam, the vibrant commercial capital of Kerala, is a bustling city that blends modernity with a rich historical heritage.The district's lush green islands and backwaters provide a tranquil escape from the city's hustle and bustle.



View More

Alappuzha

Alappuzha, known as the "Venice of the East," enchants with its intricate network of serene backwaters and houseboat cruises. The golden sandy beaches of Alappuzha offer a perfect setting for relaxation and stunning sunsets by the Arabian Sea. Alappuzha's vibrant snake boat races and traditional festivals add a colorful and festive charm to this coastal paradise.



View More

Kollam

Kollam, with its tranquil Ashtamudi Lake, is a haven for houseboat cruises and serene backwater experiences. The district's historic connections to trade and culture are reflected in its colonial-era architecture and charming temples. Kollam's unspoiled beaches and lush countryside invite visitors to explore its natural beauty and rich heritage.



View More

Trivandrum

Trivandrum, Kerala's capital city, offers a unique blend of modernity and tradition with its bustling urban life and historic temples. The city's lush green landscapes and serene beaches like Kovalam provide the perfect backdrop for relaxation. Trivandrum is home to the impressive Sri Padmanabhaswamy Temple and a variety of museums, showcasing its rich cultural heritage.



View More

Kottayam

Kottayam is renowned for its backwaters, lakes, and rubber plantations. The picturesque Vembanad Lake and unique geographical features make it a serene destination.



View More

Pathanamthitta

Pathanamthitta is famous for its religious and cultural significance, with the Sabarimala Temple drawing millions of pilgrims. The district is also known for its lush forests and wildlife sanctuaries.



View More

Thrissur

Thrissur, the cultural capital of Kerala, is known for its vibrant festivals, especially the Thrissur Pooram. The district boasts majestic temples, palaces, and a rich legacy of art and music.



View More

Malappuram

Malappuram, with its rolling hills and serene countryside, provides a tranquil escape. It's also known for its mosques, educational institutions, and a rich blend of cultures. It is famous for its teak museum in Nilambur.



View More

Kasargod

Nestled in the northernmost part of Kerala, Kasaragod boasts unspoiled beaches like Bekal, ancient forts, and a rich cultural heritage. The district is known for its serene backwaters and tranquil landscapes.
